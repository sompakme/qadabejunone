  • Manoj Muntashir is a renowned Indian lyricist, poet, and screenwriter. He is known for his exceptional work in the entertainment industry, including writing scripts for popular shows like Kaun Banega Crorepati and the Indian version of Who Wants to Be a Millionaire?.

Manoj Muntashir’s Family Background

  • Manoj was born into a Brahmin family to his father, Shiv Pratap Shukla, who works as a farmer and a pandit in weddings and worship. His mother’s name is not known, but she is a teacher. He does not have any siblings.

Manoj Muntashir’s Early Life and Education

  • He was born on 27th February 1976 in Gauriganj, Uttar Pradesh, India. Manoj attended HAL School Korwa for his high school education and pursued his bachelor’s degree from Allahabad University.

Manoj Muntashir’s Career Achievements

  • Manoj Muntashir gained popularity through his soul-stirring lyrics for Hindi songs like Galliyan, Tere Sang Yaara, Kaun Tujhe, Dil Meri Na Sune, and Phir Bhi Tumko Chaahunga.
