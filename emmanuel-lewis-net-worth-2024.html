Early Life and Career Beginnings

Emmanuel Lewis was born on March 9, 1971, in Brooklyn, New York. He began his acting career at a young age, landing his first role in a Burger King commercial. His big break came in 1983 when he was cast as the lead in the television series “Webster”.

Success with “Webster”

The sitcom “Webster” was a massive hit and catapulted Lewis to stardom. He played the title character, a young boy adopted by his godparents after his parents’ death. The show ran for six seasons, and Lewis’s performance was highly praised.

Post-Webster Career

After “Webster” ended in 1989, Lewis continued to work in the entertainment industry. He made guest appearances on various television shows and even ventured into music, releasing a single in Japan where he enjoyed a large fan base.

Entrepreneurial Ventures

Besides acting, Lewis has also dabbled in entrepreneurship. He founded his own music label, Flex Floss Entertainment, and has invested in various business ventures over the years.
