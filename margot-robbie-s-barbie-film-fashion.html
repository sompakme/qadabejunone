Margot Robbie's Barbie film fashion

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Thursday, July 4, 2024

Armani Privé

Michael Buckner/Golden Globes 2024//Getty Images

For the 2024 Golden Globes, stylist Andrew Mukamal dressed Robbie in a custom Armani Privé look inspired by the 'Superstar' Barbie from 1977. Crafted from pink shimmering sequins, the design featured the same V-neckline and tulle ruffled boa. Robbie finished the look with Manolo Blahnik heels and Lorraine Schwartz jewellery.

Prada

Dimitrios Kambouris//Getty Images

Margot Robbie perfected yet another Barbie look at the 2023 Gotham Awards; the actress channelled the "Black Magic Ensemble" doll from 1964, wearing a black Prada dress, leather gloves and an elegant sheer cape. Similar to the rest of her Barbie wardrobe, she teamed her outfit with a pair of Manolo Blahnik mules.

Sleeper

Eric Charbonneau//Getty Images

Robbie attended a special screening and Q&A in Hollywood wearing Sleeper's feathered pink pyjamas, which she styled with a coordinating woven bag and pink mules.

Bottega Veneta

Gilbert Flores//Getty Images

Andrew Mukamal revealed on Instagram that his inspiration for this look was the 'Cherry Pie Picnic' Barbie from 2015. Robbie's Deadline Contenders outfit featured almost identical turn-up jeans by Bottega Veneta, a red belt and a tie-up floral shirt, along with Manolo Blahnik mules, her favourite woven bag (this time in red) and Anita Ko jewellery.

Versace

Eric Charbonneau//Getty Images

Margot Robbie put a Barbie spin on a smart-casual look to attend a special screening and Q&A at the Warner Bros studios in LA. The actress wore a bright pink Versace blazer, a corset top and classic blue jeans. She finished the look with pointed Manolo Blahnik pumps (pink, of course) and loose soft waves.

Versace

Margot Robbie's stylist Andrew Mukamal took to Instagram to share a glimpse at some of the unseen looks from her Barbie tour wardrobe (worn for interviews or less public press appearances – scroll through the carousel to see more), including a metallic pink Versace dress and a Clueless-worthy yellow Chanel two-piece. Many of the outfits were paired with her bespoke Manolo Blahnik collection, created specifically for this tour.

"Barbie is truly iconic, so when asked to provide shoes for the wonderful Margot Robbie, whom I adore, I wanted to update some of my most successful designs that have been in my collections for decades, with a Barbie twist of course," designer Blahnik told Bazaar. "Pink is one of my absolute favourite colours – from cerise to powder pink, I love them all – and so it has been a joy to reimagine these styles in a host of pink hues."

Vivienne Westwood Couture

Karwai Tang//Getty Images

For the European Barbie premiere held in London, Margot Robbie paid tribute to the late, great British designer Vivienne Westwood, wearing a couture gown modelled on the 'Enchanted Evening' doll from 1990. The corseted dress featured a draped side train, crafted in blush pink satin and embroidered white tulle, and was accessorised with ivory opera gloves, a pearl choker and a rose brooch.

Vivienne Westwood

Stuart C. Wilson//Getty Images

Margot Robbie wore a second Vivienne Westwood ensemble while in London, attending the film's photocall in this printed skirt suit, worn with chunky platform heels and a square textured bag. She added Tiffany & Co jewellery to the look for an extra touch of sparkle.

Dilara Findikoglu

MEGA//Getty Images

On the same evening, Robbie carried on the celebrations in a red corseted mini-dress by the Turkish-British fashion designer Dilara Findikoglu. Her party look was inspired by the 1962 "Brunette Bubble Cut" Barbie, who wore a red swimsuit. The look was accessorised with Assael jewels and a Maison Ernest satin bag.

Schiaparelli Haute Couture

Frazer Harrison//Getty Images

Perhaps Margot Robbie's most glamorous Barbie look of the entire tour was this black custom Schiaparelli Haute Couture gown. For the Los Angeles premiere of the highly-anticipated film, the actress wore a design inspired by the doll's 'Solo in the Spotlight' look from 1960. It featured a glittering strapless dress with a tulle hem and a red rose accent, worn with matching opera gloves and a sheer handkerchief. She finished her red-carpet look with a Lorraine Schwartz diamond choker necklace, a bold red lip and a sleek updo.

Emilio Pucci

Hector Vivas//Getty Images

Margot Robbie's 'Totally Hair' Barbie look, based on the 1992 doll was just that; the actress wore her hair in impressive super-long waves, with an Emilio Pucci dress, pink Manolo Blahnik heels, a Chanel bag and Taffin jewellery.

Balmain

Hector Vivas//Getty Images

The actress wore a neon pink corseted Balmain mini dress with silver statement earrings and a matching chain belt, to attend the Mexico premiere.

Bottega Veneta

Courtesy of Bottega Veneta

A more casual take on the Barbie wardrobe came courtesy of this Bottega Veneta look for a day in transit, complete with baby pink Manolo Blahnik mules and Jessica McCormack diamond earrings.

Vintage Moschino

Han Myung-Gu//Getty Images

Margot Robbie kept up her vintage style streak, wearing a Moschino skirt suit from the brand's spring/summer 2015 collection to the film's press conference in Seoul. In true Barbie style, she kept her accessories perfectly matching, with a coordinating heart-shaped bag and pink Manolo Blahnik mules.

Versace

Han Myung-Gu//Getty Images

For the Barbie premiere in Seoul, Robbie turned to Versace, a favourite brand in her press tour wardrobe. This look – based on another outfit worn by the original Barbie doll – featured a pink and white skirt suit with a matching boater hat, pointed pumps, a mini bag and a glittery pink phone.

Versace

Chung Sung-Jun//Getty Images

The actress wore a second look from Versace at the premiere –a shimmering bodice and tulle skirt – also based on one of the doll's original designs. She wore her hair in the classic Barbie style of loose blonde waves, which fell perfectly over one shoulder.

Vintage Moschino

BACKGRID

In-between red carpets, Robbie was seen wearing a vintage patterned Moschino mini-dress, from the brand's spring/summer 2019 catwalk show. In true Barbie style, Robbie relied on the power of accessories with a pair of white Manolo Blahnik heels and matching white-framed sunglasses, which are becoming a regular feature in her tour wardrobe.

Vintage Versace

Another vintage moment for Margot Robbie, this time in Versace. The look – from the archives of the Italian fashion house's autumn/winter 1994 collection – perfectly captures Barbie's aesthetic with a metallic pink pleated skirt and high-neck knit, paired with purple socks, white heels and Jessica McCormack jewellery.

Vintage Versace

James Gourley//Getty Images

While on stage at a Barbie fan event in Sydney, – joined by co-stars America Ferrera, Issa Rae and director Greta Gerwig – Robbie added a pair of white sunglasses to her vintage Versace look.

Vintage Versace

James Gourley//Getty Images

Yet another vintage Versace moment for the actress, who stepped out on the pink carpet in a blush chainmail Versace mini-dress, while attending a Barbie party in Sydney. The dress was originally worn by the model Claudia Schiffer in 1994.
