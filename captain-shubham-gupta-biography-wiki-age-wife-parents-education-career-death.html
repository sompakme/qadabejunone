Captain Shubham Gupta Biography, Wiki, Age, Wife, Parents, Education, Career &amp; Death

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Tuesday, July 9, 2024

Captain Shubham Gupta (born 9 October 1997 – died 23 November 2023, Aged: 27 years old) was an Indian Army officer who was martyred in a terrorist attack in Jammu and Kashmir on 23 November 2023. He was leading a team of soldiers in a counter-insurgency operation in the Pulwama district when they came under heavy fire from the militants.

He fought valiantly and killed two terrorists before succumbing to his injuries. He was posthumously awarded the Shaurya Chakra, the third-highest gallantry award in India, for his courage and devotion to duty.

Family Background and Education

Captain Shubham Gupta was born on 9 October 1997 in Jaipur, Rajasthan. His father, Basant Gupta, is a businessman, and his mother, Pushpa Gupta, is a homemaker. He has a younger brother, Rishabh Gupta, who is pursuing engineering. Shubham was interested in joining the army since his childhood and was inspired by his grandfather, who was also an army veteran.

He completed his schooling at St. Xavier’s School, Jaipur, and excelled in academics and sports. He was the head boy of his school and participated in various co-curricular activities. He was also a National Cadet Corps (NCC) cadet and attended several camps and competitions.

He scored 96% in his class 12 board exams and secured admission to the prestigious Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Delhi. He pursued B.Tech in Computer Science and Engineering from IIT Delhi and graduated with honors in 2019. He was also a part of the Entrepreneurship Cell and the Coding Club of IIT Delhi and won several awards and accolades for his projects and innovations.

Career and Achievements

After completing his graduation, Captain Shubham had many lucrative jobs offers from multinational companies, but he chose to follow his passion and join the Indian Army. He cleared the Combined Defence Services (CDS) examination and joined the Indian Military Academy (IMA) in Dehradun in 2019.

He underwent rigorous training and emerged as one of the best cadets of his batch. He was commissioned as a Lieutenant in the 4th Battalion of the Rajputana Rifles in 2020 and was posted in Jammu and Kashmir. He proved his mettle as a young and dynamic officer and led many successful operations against the terrorists.

He was promoted to the rank of Captain in 2021 and was given the command of a company. He was known for his bravery, professionalism, and leadership skills among his peers and subordinates. He was also popular among the local people and was involved in many civic action programs and welfare activities. He was fond of reading, writing, and playing chess in his spare time.

Death and Legacy

On 23 November 2023, Captain Shubham Gupta was leading a team of 10 soldiers in a cordon and search operation in the Litter area of Pulwama district, based on a tip-off about the presence of some terrorists. As they approached the suspected hideout, they were ambushed by a group of heavily armed militants. A fierce gunfight ensued, in which Captain Gupta displayed exemplary courage and tactical acumen.

He engaged the terrorists in close-quarter combat and killed two of them. He also ensured the safety of his men and evacuated the injured soldiers to a safer place. However, he sustained multiple bullet injuries in the process and succumbed to them on the spot. He was 27 years old. His mortal remains were flown to his hometown, Jaipur, where he was given a state funeral with full military honors. He was cremated with a 21-gun salute and his ashes were immersed in the Ganga river.

He is survived by his parents, brother, and wife, Neha Gupta, whom he married in February 2023. He was posthumously awarded the Shaurya Chakra, the third-highest gallantry award in India, for his conspicuous bravery and supreme sacrifice in the face of the enemy. He was also conferred the Chief of Army Staff Commendation Card and the Governor of Rajasthan Commendation Card. He is remembered as a hero and an inspiration by his family, friends, colleagues, and the nation.
