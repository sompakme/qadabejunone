How To Get From Austin Airport To Downtown Austin

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Sunday, July 14, 2024
People are currently reading this guide.

Howdy Partner, You Just Landed in ATX! (But How Do You Get Out of This Metal Bird?)

So you just touched down in Austin, Texas. Yeehaw! You're practically smelling the breakfast tacos and dodging tumbleweeds already (well, maybe not the tumbleweeds, but definitely the delicious breakfast tacos). But hold your horses (or should we say, breakfast tacos?), you're not quite in the heart of the action yet. That's where this little guide comes in, sugar. We'll get you from the tarmac to Sixth Street faster than you can say "two-step."

Buckle Up, Buttercup: Here Are Your Transportation Options

Getting downtown from Austin-Bergstrom International Airport (fancy name, huh?) is a breeze. You've got more choices than boots at a honky-tonk. Here's a rundown of your best bets:

  • The Six-Shooter (Taxi): This is the classic, the John Wayne of transportation. It'll get you there fast and in style (well, as stylish as being stuck in traffic can be), but it won't be the friendliest on your wallet. Think "luxury" not "budget-friendly."

  • The Trusty Steed (Ride Sharing): Hop in a ride-sharing app and let a friendly local chauffeur you around. It's usually quicker than a taxi and can be more affordable, especially if you split the fare with some fellow travelers. Just remember surge pricing can turn your ride into a rodeo!

  • The Public Gallop (Bus): For the adventurous soul (or the penny-pincher), there's always the Capital Metro bus. Route 20 takes you straight downtown for a song (and by song, we mean a very reasonable bus fare). It might not be the fastest option, but hey, you get to see the city and save some cash!

  • The Lone Star Chariot (Shuttle): Several shared shuttles operate between the airport and downtown hotels. This is a good option if you don't want to deal with hailing a cab or navigating the bus system. Just be prepared to make a few extra stops along the way.

Still on the Fence? Here's a Quick Decision-Maker

  • Need speed? Taxi or ride-sharing might be your best bet (as long as there's no surge pricing).
  • Ballin' on a budget? The bus is your champion.
  • Traveling with a posse? Look into a shared van or shuttle.

Remember: No matter which mode of transport you choose, sit back, relax, and take in the Texas scenery. You're about to experience some true Austin hospitality!

Howdy, FAQ Partner!

You've got questions, we've (probably) got answers. Here's a quick rundown of some commonly asked questions:

How to catch a taxi at the airport? Head to the ground transportation area outside baggage claim. Look for the official taxi queue and hop in line.

How to use ride-sharing apps at the airport? Follow the app's instructions to request a ride. There might be designated pick-up zones, so check the app for details.

How often do the buses run? Capital Metro Route 20 runs frequently, usually every 15-20 minutes. You can check the Capital Metro website for real-time schedules.

How much does it cost to get downtown? Prices vary depending on your chosen method. Taxis are the most expensive, followed by ride-sharing (depending on surge pricing). The bus is the most affordable option.

How long does it take to get downtown? Traffic can be unpredictable, but generally a taxi or ride-sharing app will get you there in 15-30 minutes. The bus might take a bit longer, around 35-45 minutes.
