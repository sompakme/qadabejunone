ITV The Bay: Marsha Thomasons net worth, husband who works in Hollywood and forgotten role in Pri

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Thursday, August 1, 2024

Season three of The Bay airs on ITV tonight (January 12), which sees another investigation underway with a new family liaison officer on the scene.

Morven Christie decided to call it quits as DS Lisa Armstrong after two seasons on the hit crime drama.

The Scottish star is being replaced by Jamaican-born actress Marsha Thomason, who plays the leading role of DS Jenn Townsend in the new series. So, where might you recognise her from?

Her best-known roles have been as Nessa Holt in Las Vegas, Sharon Pearce in Playing the Field, Naomi Dorrit in Lost and Diana Berrigan in White Collar.

One of her most recent parts was as Francine Bridge in the Sky drama Cobra, while you might also recognise her from her brief role as Janice in episode five of Prime Suspect back in 1996.

Thomason, 45, is married to Craig Sykes, who has previously worked in Hollywood as a lighting technician.

The pair married in 2009 in Malibu, California, and the pair share baby daughter Tallulah.

Unlike other crime dramas, The Bay focuses on the role of the family liaison officer who is assigned to families who have lost a loved one.

Morven Christie's decision to leave could have spelled the end for the crime drama - but producers have taken it as a new opportunity.

Speaking to Express.co.uk, screenwriter Daragh Carville said: "Obviously, it was a big change. You know, when when we learned that Morgan was moving on, that was a huge kind of shift.

"And personally, I felt it was very sad because she's so brilliant in the show and she brought so much to that character and I love writing for, for her and for Lisa.

"But at the same time, it was kind of an opportunity to sort of reinvent things and to kind of refresh the show."

He continued: "To see what else you know we could do with this world, with these stories.

"So it was kind of a combination of things. On the one hand, it is really exciting to welcome Marsha.

"It's very much the same show. It's very much the same bag again. But it's almost like a new show too. So that was really exciting."
